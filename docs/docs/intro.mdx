# AI Education — Practical AI Skills for Professionals

A self-paced curriculum for building real AI fluency — designed in three progressive
phases, each building on the last, each immediately practical.

:::tip Who this is for & what you'll get out of it
**Who this is for:** busy professionals — no technical background, no programming, no
prior AI experience required. If you write, research, analyze, plan, or manage for a
living, this is for you.

**The goal:** by the end of Phases 1–3 you'll be able to —

- **Explain how AI actually works** — tokens, context windows, hallucination, agents —
  well enough to make good decisions with it.
- **Use the Claude app effectively** in your day-to-day work: documents, research,
  analysis, and planning.
- **Use AI responsibly** — knowing what not to share, how to verify output, and how to
  bring your team along.

The optional appendix goes under the hood for readers who want the technical depth.
:::

:::info Adopting this for your team?
This curriculum is open source and designed to be forked and adapted — swap in your
own examples, policies, and branding. See the
[README](https://github.com/alexkhadiwala/ai-education#readme) to get started.
:::

## Take the self-assessments in Claude

Each Phase 1 and Phase 2 module ends with an interview-style self-assessment that runs
**inside the Claude app** — Claude asks a few open-ended questions, scores your answers,
and builds you a personal review plan.

**One-time setup:** [download the assessment skill](/downloads/ai-assessment-skill.zip),
upload it in Claude under **Settings → Capabilities → Skills**, then say:

```text
assess me on phase 1 module 1     # one module
assess me on phase 1              # all three Phase 1 modules
assess me on phase 2 module 3     # any Phase 2 module
```

Paste the results block Claude gives you into the [Assessments](/assessments) page to
track your progress. Full instructions <a href="/assessments#setup">here</a>; preview every
question on the [Question Bank](/question-bank) page.

## Phase 1 — AI Essentials

**Start here.** Three focused modules covering the foundation you need to work
effectively with AI tools. Each module is ~45 minutes and includes hands-on exercises.

*By the end of Phase 1 you'll be able to explain — to a colleague — what a token,
context window, hallucination, and agent are, and why each one matters for the work
you do.*

| Module | What you'll learn |
|--------|------------------|
| [**1. How AI Actually Works**](./phase-1/how-ai-works) | Tokens, parameters, training vs. inference — the mental models that make everything else make sense |
| [**2. Using AI Effectively**](./phase-1/using-ai-effectively) | Context windows, prompting techniques, hallucination, RAG |
| [**3. AI in Your Workday**](./phase-1/ai-in-your-workflow) | Assistants, agents, tools, security, practical patterns |

Complete Phase 1 in order — each module builds on the last.

---

## Phase 2 — Claude in Practice

**Do this after Phase 1.** Five practical modules on using the Claude app effectively
in your daily work. Hands-on, opinionated, immediately applicable.

*By the end of Phase 2 you'll be able to brief Claude well, set up Projects for your
recurring work, produce real documents, extend Claude with connectors and skills, and
run reliable everyday workflows.*

| Module | What you'll learn |
|--------|------------------|
| [**1. Getting Started with Claude**](./phase-2/claude-essentials) | The app, writing a good first message, attachments, models, chat hygiene |
| [**2. Projects, Memory & Custom Instructions**](./phase-2/projects-and-instructions) | Persistent context: Projects, knowledge, instructions, preferences, styles |
| [**3. Creating Documents & Artifacts**](./phase-2/creating-with-artifacts) | Drafting, iterating, and exporting real documents, spreadsheets, and decks |
| [**4. Connectors, MCP & Skills**](./phase-2/connectors-and-skills) | Extending Claude safely — including installing this course's assessment skill |
| [**5. Everyday Workflows**](./phase-2/everyday-workflows) | Document review, research, data analysis, meetings — and how to migrate your work over, task by task |

Phase 2 modules can be taken in any order, but Module 1 is the best starting point.

---

## Phase 3 — Working Confidently with AI

**Do this after Phase 2.** The judgment layer: five modules on the difference between
using AI and using it well — safely, verifiably, and across a team.

| Module | What you'll learn |
|--------|------------------|
| [**1. Confidentiality, Privacy & Sensitive Data**](./phase-3/confidentiality-and-privacy) | What happens to your data, what never to paste, plans and policies |
| [**2. Verifying AI Output & Managing Risk**](./phase-3/verifying-ai-output) | Stakes-based verification, fabricated citations, when not to use AI |
| [**3. Choosing AI Tools & Plans**](./phase-3/choosing-tools-and-plans) | Plan tiers, costs, and comparing vendors without the hype |
| [**4. Bringing AI to Your Team**](./phase-3/bringing-ai-to-your-team) | Rollout, policy, training, champions, and measuring adoption |
| [**5. Staying Current**](./phase-3/staying-current) | Keeping up without drowning — a sustainable information diet |
